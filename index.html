The Challenge

The Problem

Head-Level Blind Zone

White canes leave a dangerous gap

Traditional white canes are excellent tools — but they can only detect obstacles at ground level. Objects at head or chest height, such as open cabinet doors, hanging branches, or scaffolding bars, are completely invisible to the cane.

This gap in spatial awareness creates real daily risks: dangerous collisions, loss of confidence, and reduced independence for millions of blind and visually impaired people worldwide.

  • Cannot detect head-level obstacles
  • No alert for protruding objects
  • Limited range in complex environments
  • No real-time environmental awareness
The Answer

Our Solution

HD Camera Front-mounted module
Edge AI On-device inference
Voice + Haptics Dual alert system

Aurora Vision is an AI-powered wearable that fills the critical blind spot left by traditional mobility aids.

A front-mounted camera captures the environment continuously. An embedded AI model detects obstacles in real time — at any height — and immediately notifies the user through clear voice guidance and vibration alerts.

The result: a safer, more confident, more independent way to navigate the world — no matter where the obstacle is.

Detects obstacles at all heights
Works indoors and outdoors
Lightweight, wearable design
Companion mobile app included
Capabilities

Built-In Features

Every component of Aurora Vision is engineered to maximize safety, usability, and independence.

Real-Time Detection

The camera continuously scans the environment and the AI identifies obstacles at head, chest, and ground level — within milliseconds.

Voice Guidance

Clear, natural voice alerts tell the user what obstacle is ahead and its direction — left, right, or center — in real time.

Vibration Alerts

Haptic feedback provides a secondary, discreet alert channel — useful in noisy environments or when the user prefers silent mode.

AI-Powered Vision

Embedded computer vision models classify obstacles, estimate distance, and adapt to changing environments — all without an internet connection.

The Process

How It Works

01

Camera Captures the Environment

A compact HD camera on the glasses frame continuously records the scene ahead — at eye level and beyond.

02

AI Detects Obstacles Instantly

A trained neural network processes the video feed on-device, identifying obstacles and estimating their distance — in real time.

03

User Receives Voice & Vibration Alerts

Immediate audio cues and haptic pulses notify the user of the obstacle — direction, type, and proximity — keeping them safe and informed.
